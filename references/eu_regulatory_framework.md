# EU Regulatory Framework — Citation Map

Use this map to decide which instrument to cite for a given claim. Full HTML copies in `assets/eu_ppp_regulations/`. Always cite CELEX and the specific Article/Annex point.

## Cite when discussing approval of active substances, placing PPP on market

- **Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009** [CELEX:32009R1107]
  - Art. 4: approval criteria for active substances
  - Art. 14-20: renewal of approval
  - Art. 24: candidates for substitution
  - Art. 28-39: product authorisation
  - Art. 40-42: mutual recognition
  - Art. 43: re-authorisation following AS renewal
  - Art. 50: comparative assessment for substitution candidates
  - Art. 59-63: data protection (and reference product principle)
  - Annex II: criteria for approval (incl. ED cut-off via Reg 2018/605)

## Cite when discussing data requirements

- **Regulation (EU) No 283/2013** [CELEX:32013R0283] — active substances
- **Regulation (EU) No 284/2013** [CELEX:32013R0284] — PPP
- **Regulation (EU) 2022/1439** [CELEX:32022R1439] — amends 283/2013, microorganisms
- **Regulation (EU) 2022/1441** [CELEX:32022R1441] — amends 284/2013, microorganisms

When citing 283/2013 or 284/2013, name the Part (A chemicals, B microorganisms) and the data point ID (e.g. "283/2013 Annex Part A, point 7.1.4" for short-term oral tox).

## Cite when discussing evaluation principles

- **Regulation (EU) No 546/2011** [CELEX:32011R0546] — uniform principles
- **Regulation (EU) 2022/1438** [CELEX:32022R1438] — amends 546/2011 for microorganisms

## Cite when discussing approved substances list

- **Regulation (EU) No 540/2011** [CELEX:32011R0540] — Annex lists approved active substances; continually amended. Cite the consolidated text and the specific implementing regulation that added/removed the substance.

## Cite when discussing renewal procedure

- **Regulation (EU) 2020/1740** [CELEX:32020R1740] — current renewal procedure (replaces 844/2012)
- **Regulation (EU) No 844/2012** [CELEX:32012R0844] — earlier procedure, repealed; cite only for historical renewals predating 2021

## Cite when discussing endocrine disruptor criteria

- **Regulation (EU) 2018/605** [CELEX:32018R0605] — ED scientific criteria
- ECHA/EFSA Guidance for identification of EDs (2018) — methodological
- Reg 283/2013 as amended — data requirements for ED assessment

## Cite when discussing MRLs

- **Regulation (EC) No 396/2005** [CELEX:32005R0396]
  - Art. 6-11: MRL setting
  - Art. 12: review of existing MRLs
  - Art. 14-17: dietary risk assessment
  - Annex I: products covered
  - Annex II: established MRLs
  - Annex III: temporary MRLs
  - Annex IV: substances not requiring MRL
  - Annex V: default 0.01 mg/kg

## Cite when discussing adjacent chemicals legislation

- **Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP)** [CELEX:32008R1272] — classification of active substances and PPP
- **Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006 (REACH)** [CELEX:32006R1907] — co-formulants, safeners (interface only; PPP active substances exempt from registration but Annex XVII restrictions apply)
- **Regulation (EU) No 528/2012 (BPR)** [CELEX:32012R0528] — biocides (parallel regime; cite for cross-use comparisons)
- **Regulation (EU) No 649/2012 (PIC)** [CELEX:32012R0649] — export of banned/restricted PPP

## Citation form

Inline: `Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009, Art. 4(1) [CELEX:32009R1107]`

Reference list (EFSA style):
> European Commission, 2009. Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 21 October 2009 concerning the placing of plant protection products on the market and repealing Council Directives 79/117/EEC and 91/414/EEC. OJ L 309, 24.11.2009, p. 1-50. CELEX:32009R1107.

For amended consolidated texts, add the consolidation date: `consolidated text of [date]`.

## Verifying in-force status

Before citing, confirm:
1. Instrument is in force (not repealed)
2. Specific Article/Annex point has not been amended since the version you are reading
3. CELEX number is the original adoption, not a consolidated version reference

The HTML copies in `assets/eu_ppp_regulations/` are point-in-time snapshots. For Article-level citations on contested points, cross-check the current EUR-Lex consolidated text via WebFetch.
