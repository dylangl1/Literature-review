# Literature review depth models

| Depth | Summary | Research question | Search strategy | Screening | Quality appraisal | Data extraction | Synthesis | Reporting outputs | Typical use |
|-------|---------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Full** | Full structured · Systematic · Auditable | Formal PICO/PECO framing; PROSPERO protocol registered before search | 3+ databases plus grey literature; Boolean logic fully documented | Dual independent reviewers; inter-rater reliability (Cohen's kappa) reported | Validated tool (CAMARADES, Klimisch, OHAT); risk of bias per study | Pre-defined extraction form; structured evidence tables per study | Weight-of-evidence synthesis; narrative per endpoint; PRISMA flow | Full PRISMA report; published protocol; formal bias assessment | Regulatory submissions, EFSA WoE assessments, peer-reviewed publication |
| **Condensed** | Quick review · Targeted · Efficient | Targeted scope defined; no protocol registration required | 2–3 databases; bounded date range; scope limitation stated explicitly | Single reviewer; inclusion/exclusion rationale clearly documented | Qualitative quality flagging; no formal appraisal tool required | Abbreviated summary matrix; key endpoints and effect levels captured | Direct concise synthesis; conclusions follow immediately from evidence summary | Lean PRISMA flow; mandatory limitations section with scope constraints | Internal briefings, data gap checks, scoping ahead of a full review |
| **Adapted** | Dynamic · Structured | Per-arm framing; comparison axes defined upfront before searching | Parallel arm-specific searches; shared base terms plus arm-specific terms | Per-arm inclusion criteria; criteria can legitimately differ across arms | Arm-appropriate tools; appraisal methods can vary across comparators | Unified cross-arm extraction schema; enables direct comparison across arms | Convergence/divergence mapping across arms; dynamic rather than linear | Custom comparison framework; tabular or matrix outputs standard | Active substance comparisons, tiering analysis, cross-compound regulatory profiling |
